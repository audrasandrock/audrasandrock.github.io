Our Work

Quality Curb, Inc. is an extruded curb company.
We have been installing concrete curbs for landscaping and parking
lots since 1998. The process of our extruded concrete curbing is done
either by an electric or gas driven machine. The curbing mix
is compacted through the use of a machine with a mechanically
driven ram into a slip-form for extrusion into final
structural design of the curbing border. Our unique process allows
installation to be fast, clean and less
expensive than traditional methods.

Installation

The sod cutting and ground preparation is completed first making the landscape ready for curb installation. This is done using a sod-cutting machine to cut an approx. 12" wide strip of sod about 1-2 inches deep, and the crew using flat nose shovels to remove the grass, and then leveling the ground. Once the ground preparation is completed we are ready for installation. We do ask that you identify any telephone or cable lines running in the area to be curbed.The concrete is then mixed on a self-contained trailer, and if color is desired
it is added to the exact measurement for the job. The mixture of concrete sand, Portland cement, and reinforced fibers is what creates our strong durable curb.

Once the concrete is mixed it is wheeled to the site, fed into the curbing machine and extruded into a continuous border for your landscaped area. The crew will then finish out and beautify the curb depending on the type of curb you are installing, either using trowels or a stamping process to finish the installation. As an added precaution to ground movement, control joints are cut into the curb approximate every 12-18 inches to add flexibility to a very strong reinforced curb. The installation of your curb is a 1-day process, and when the crew is completed your landscape will be as clean, or cleaner, than when they arrived.

FAQ

How will I replace my exsisting edging with curbing affect my landscape?The installer will remove the existing edging, and cut an approx. 12 inch wide strip of sod away from the outside edge of your existing landscaping. This will allow them to extrude the curb against your current edge or wherever your new curb is designed to go. If you have any sprinkler heads on the outside edge of your existing landscape, they may need to be relocated. If they are inside your edging the curbing installation process will usually not affect them. The installer will ensure proper drainage from the area to be enclosed and also see that any gates will open properly, in areas where curbing is installed.

Are forms set to do concrete lanscape curbing?No, the curbing machine extrudes and compresses the concrete into a continuous concrete border, which makes the installation faster, cleaner and a 1-day process possible.

How long does the process take?Depending on the type and footage of the installation, it normally takes only 1 day. On the day of your curb's installation, it is extremely important to keep any children and pets away from the curbing until it can adequately cure. We issue an instruction sheet for our customer's use, in the care and maintenance of the newly installed curbing, when the job is completed

Do you use reinforcement in your curbing?Yes, reinforced fibers are used in our curbing as they prevent separation of the curb.

Will the color fade over the years?Yes, all colors bleach in sunlight, even if integral colors are used in the mixture. There are sealers that may prevent some fading. Resealing is good maintenance practice.

Will I be able to mow up against my curbing? Your curbing will be very strong and will withstand your mowers' weight to ride along the edge of the curbing. Since your mower will not damage your curbing, this will allow you to mow right up against it, resulting in less time used weed eating around your landscaped areas. A clever idea given by a customer during feedback, is to use some type of weed-killer spray along the outside of the newly installed curb (after curing), to keep a clear area, almost eliminating weed eating.

Will the curbing break? All concrete will crack, especially when droughts and/or ground subsidence occurs. To control cracking, control joints every 12-18 inches are added to allow your curbing to expand and contract. In addition, fiberglass fibers are always added to stop separation of the curb.
